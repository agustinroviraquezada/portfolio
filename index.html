Sobre mí

I am a biomedical engineer working at the intersection of healthcare data, machine learning and artificial intelligence.

My background combines research in neural signal processing, experience with clinical systems and applied data science on large-scale healthcare datasets.

Early in my career I worked in neuroscience research groups at Universitat Pompeu Fabra and the Consejo Superior de Investigaciones Científicas, where I focused on analysing brain signals such as EEG and fNIRS. During this time I developed signal processing pipelines and applied machine learning techniques to classify neural activity in brain–computer interface experiments.

I later joined Philips, working on the deployment and support of digital pathology and radiology systems in hospitals and clinical laboratories. This experience gave me first-hand exposure to healthcare IT infrastructure, medical imaging systems and the operational workflows that generate clinical data.

Currently I work as a Clinical Data Scientist at Savana, where I analyse large-scale real-world clinical datasets generated through Natural Language Processing applied to electronic health records. My work focuses on designing data analysis pipelines, validating NLP-derived clinical databases and generating insights for real-world evidence studies and clinical research. My interests are focused on the development of applied AI systems for healthcare, particularly in:

• Clinical Natural Language Processing
• Machine Learning applied to healthcare data
• Real-world evidence analytics
• AI systems for medical text and clinical knowledge extraction

I am currently expanding my expertise in applied machine learning, transformer-based NLP models and large language models for clinical applications. I am particularly interested in emerging approaches such as retrieval-augmented generation and agentic AI systems built on top of large language models to support clinical research and healthcare analytics.
