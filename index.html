Sobre mí

Currently at Savana, I analyse clinical datasets derived from NLP processing of electronic health records — validating NLP systems, building data pipelines, and running statistical analyses for real-world evidence (RWE) studies. My work spans the full data science lifecycle: SQL extraction → EDA → model development → interpretability (SHAP).

Previously at Philips (Digital Pathology & Radiology), I worked on the deployment of clinical imaging systems in hospitals and developed data analysis scripts to optimise clinical workflows, giving me direct exposure to healthcare IT infrastructure and the operational data it generates.

Earlier, as a Research Intern at CSIC & Universitat Pompeu Fabra, I built ML models (SVM) for EEG signal classification and designed multimodal neural signal acquisition experiments (EEG, fNIRS).

My current focus: applied AI for healthcare — clinical NLP, transformer-based models, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and agentic AI systems for clinical research and medical knowledge extraction.
